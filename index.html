Classroom Communication Aide

The Project

The Classroom Communication Aide is an academic project developed as part of CSCI 436/536: Technology for Social Good at Western Washington University. Our mission is to create an ICT to address SDG 4 by bridging the communication gap between multilingual K-12 learners and their educators.

X Languages Supported
Custom Request Buttons
Real-time Translation

The Problem

In todays educational system, language barriers present significant challenges to effective classroom communication. Teachers often struggle to convey information to students who speak different primary languages, while students may feel isolaed or unable to express their needs and questions.

Our extensive research confirmed this, as we studied national data and focused on interviewing local teachers, and other stakeholders.

Our Solution

We decided to develop an accessible web application that could be used across a wide range of devices. We developed our solution to focus on using visual aids, to begin introducing the association between objects and their pronounciations. We focused on improving the interactions between multilingual students and their teachers, as we decided to implement real time two-way translaton to help facilitate communications.

Compatible Design

Accessible on any device, ensuring teachers can communicate effectively anywhere in the classroom

Visual Aides

Icon-based requests to help students build association between images and english words

Two-Way Translation

Students and teachers can communicate, each in their native langauge, promoting genuine dialogue

Research Methodology

Systematic Literature Review

We have two read two paper each that are relevant to our project. In total we have eight papers. Then, for each paper, we need to answer the following questions:

  • Title of the paper
  • Weblink to the paper
  • Publication year
  • Name of the conference or journal
  • Author list
  • Name of the Institute of the first author
  • Abstract of the paper
  • What is the research goal of this paper?
  • What are the major contributions of this paper?
  • How does your project relate to this paper?
  • How does your project differ from this paper?
  • What aspects of this paper did you like most? Why?
  • If you have to do the same research, how will you do it differently? Why?
